# 📌 Overview

This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of survey responses collected from data professionals across multiple countries. It transforms raw survey data into meaningful business insights by highlighting salary trends, demographic distributions, programming language preferences, and workplace satisfaction metrics.

The project demonstrates practical skills in **Power BI, Power Query, DAX, Data Cleaning, Data Modeling, KPI Design, and Business Intelligence.**

---

# 🎯 Business Questions Answered

✔ Which countries contributed the highest number of survey responses?

✔ Which data profession earns the highest salary?

✔ How do salaries compare between male and female professionals?

✔ Which programming language is the most popular among data professionals?

✔ Are professionals satisfied with their salary?

✔ How satisfied are professionals with their work-life balance?

✔ What is the average age of professionals participating in the survey?

# 📈 Dashboard Components

## 🌍 Country Distribution

The treemap visualizes the geographical distribution of survey respondents.

### Insights

- 🇺🇸 United States has the highest participation.
- 🇮🇳 India ranks second.
- 🇬🇧 United Kingdom and 🇨🇦 Canada contribute a moderate number of responses.
- Other countries are grouped together for better visualization.

---

## 💰 Average Salary by Gender

A donut chart compares the average salary earned by male and female professionals.

### Insight

The average salary distribution is relatively balanced, suggesting no major salary gap within the surveyed dataset.

## 💼 Average Salary by Job Title

Ranks job roles according to average annual salary.

| Rank | Job Role |
|------|-----------|
| 🥇 | Data Scientist |
| 🥈 | Data Engineer |
| 🥉 | Data Architect |
| 4 | Other |
| 5 | Data Analyst |
| 6 | Database Developer |
| 7 | Student / Looking for Work |

### Observation

Data Scientists earn the highest average salary, while students and job seekers naturally report the lowest compensation.

---

## 💻 Favorite Programming Language

The clustered bar chart highlights language preferences across different job roles.

### Findings

- Python dominates the data industry.
- R remains popular among analysts.
- JavaScript and Java have relatively low adoption.
- C/C++ is used only by a small percentage of respondents.

---

## 😊 Work-Life Balance

**Average Rating**

> **5.74 / 10**

Professionals report moderate satisfaction regarding work-life balance.

---

## 💵 Salary Happiness

**Average Rating**

> **4.27 / 10**

Salary satisfaction is noticeably lower than work-life balance satisfaction, indicating opportunities for organizations to improve compensation strategies.

---

# 📌 Key Insights

### Salary

- Data Scientists are the highest-paid professionals.
- Data Engineers closely follow.
- Students earn the least.

### Programming

- Python is the industry's preferred language.
- R is widely used for analytics.
- Traditional programming languages play a smaller role.

### Workforce

- Most respondents come from the United States.
- Average participant age is approximately **30 years**.

### Satisfaction

- Work-life balance scores higher than salary satisfaction.
- Compensation remains one of the biggest concerns among professionals.
